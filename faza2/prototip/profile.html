Elden Ring ★★★★✰

You have to have a very high Elden to understand Elden Ring. The Ring is extremely subtle, and without a solid grasp of theoretical Elden most of the Ring will go over a typical gamer's head. There's also Elden's nihilistic outlook, which is deftly woven into his characterisation - his personal philosophy draws heavily from Narodnaya Volya literature, for instance. The fans understand this stuff; they have the intellectual capacity to truly appreciate the depths of these Ring, to realise that they're not just Elden - they say something deep about RING. As a consequence people who dislike Elden Ring truly ARE Elden - of course they wouldn't appreciate, for instance, the Ring in Elden's existential catchphrase "Elden Ring" which itself is a cryptic reference to Turgenev's Russian epic Fathers and Sons. I'm gaming right now just imagining one of those addlepated gamers scratching their heads in confusion as Hideo Kojima's genius wit unfolds itself on their monitor screens. What Elden..how I Ring them.
